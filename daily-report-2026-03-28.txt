  the "leaven" of Egypt — worldly mindsets — and receive renewed vision.

• DIVINE REALIGNMENT: God is aligning His people with His calendar, His 
  purposes, and His timing.

This is the Shabbat immediately preceding Passover. It is called "HaGadol" 
(The Great) because:

1. The Israelites took the Passover lamb on this day (10 Nisan) in Egypt
2. The Egyptians could not prevent them — a demonstration of God's protection
3. The Haftarah (Malachi 3) speaks of the "Great Day of the LORD" that comes 
   like a refiner's fire

HAFTARAH KEY VERSE (Malachi 3:23-24 / 4:5-6):
"Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great 
and terrible day of the LORD. And he shall turn the heart of the fathers to 
the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers."

WHAT SHOULD BE TAUGHT THIS SHABBAT:
• The significance of taking the lamb on 10 Nisan — separation and examination
• The connection between the first redemption (Egypt) and final redemption
• The role of Elijah in preparing the way — preparing our households
