## Discovery Call Framework

**Opening:**
"Thanks for taking the time. I'd love to understand what you're working on and see if there's a fit. I'll ask some questions, and at the end, I'll let you know honestly if I think I can help - and if not, I'll try to point you in the right direction. Sound good?"

**Key Questions:**
1. "What kind of AI or automation requests are you getting from clients right now?"
2. "How are you currently handling those requests?"
3. "What would success look like in 6 months - more revenue per client, new service offering, something else?"
4. "What's prevented you from adding AI capabilities already?"
5. "What kind of budget have you allocated for this?"
6. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"

**Closing:**
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing..."
"I think [can/cannot] help. Here's why..."
"Next step would be [proposal/demo/intro to someone else]"

## Red Flags to Watch For

⚠️ "Can you work for revenue share?" - Usually means no budget commitment
⚠️ "I need this yesterday" - Poor planning, scope creep incoming
⚠️ "It's simple, just a quick chatbot" - Never simple
⚠️ Won't share budget range - Time-waster signal
⚠️ Multiple decision makers not aligned - Approval nightmare
⚠️ "My last developer ghosted me" - Check if the problem was really the dev
