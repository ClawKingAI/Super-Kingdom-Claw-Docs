- **Healing** — The piercing that brings healing

In the current crisis, we see God potentially:
- Purifying His people through trial

- Calling the Church to discernment and preparation

This portion contains Moses' encounter with God after the golden calf incident. Moses asks to see God's glory, and God proclaims His character: "The LORD, the LORD, a God merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love and faithfulness" (Exodus 34:6). God asks, "Son of man, can these bones live?" and then commands Ezekiel to prophesy to the bones. They come together, receive breath, and become "an exceedingly great army." This is read during Passover because it speaks of Israel's national resurrection—a theme connected to Passover's deliverance.

1. **God's Glory and Mercy** — Even after Israel's failure with the golden calf, God reveals His merciful character
2. **Resurrection Power** — The dry bones represent Israel's apparent death and future restoration
3. **The Breath of God** — Just as God breathed life into Adam, He breathes life into His people
4. **New Covenant Foreshadowing** — God's promise to "cleanse" His people points to Messiah's work

## THE MONTH OF NISSAN TEACHING

### The Piercing That Has Happened

In the first month, Nisan, we remember that deliverance required a piercing—the lamb's blood was applied to the doorposts with hyssop. This piercing of the lamb's body and the application of its blood pointed forward to Messiah's piercing for our transgressions (Isaiah 53:5). The Hebrew letter for Nisan is **Hei** (ה), which represents the breath of God—divine revelation breathed into human history.

**What this means now:** The piercing we experience in our own lives—trials, losses, difficulties—are not random. They are the place where God breathes His revelation. Just as Israel's bondage was pierced by God's judgments on Egypt, our bondages are pierced by God's working in our lives.

### Fresh Manna and Fresh Fire

The Exodus generation received manna for the first time in the month of Nisan. God provided fresh bread from heaven. This speaks of:
- **Daily dependence** — Manna couldn't be stored; it had to be gathered fresh each day
- **God's sufficiency** — In the wilderness, God provided exactly what was needed
- **New mercies every morning** — Lamentations 3:22-23

**What this means now:** In this season of crisis, God is calling us back to daily dependence. We cannot rely on yesterday's provision. We need fresh manna—fresh encounters with God's Word and presence.

### Healing: Heart, Mind, Body, Spirit

Nisan is associated with the tribe of Judah, which means "praise." Praise is healing. When we praise God in the midst of crisis, something happens:
- **Heart healing** — Praise releases us from bitterness and fear
- **Mind healing** — Praise shifts our focus from problems to the Problem-Solver
- **Body healing** — "A cheerful heart is good medicine" (Proverbs 17:22)
- **Spirit healing** — Praise connects us to God's presence

**What this means now:** Passover is a time of healing. Just as Israel left Egypt with their health restored (Psalm 105:37), God wants to heal His people today.

### Regaining Strength for Higher Knowledge

The Exodus required strength—the strength to leave everything familiar, to face the unknown, to trust God completely. That strength came from knowing God's character (Exodus 34:6-7).

**Higher knowledge** in Scripture is the knowledge of God Himself (Philippians 3:10). This surpasses mere information about God; it is intimate encounter.

**What this means now:** The current crisis is an invitation to deeper knowledge of God. As we see nations in turmoil, we are called to know the One who "removes kings and sets up kings" (Daniel 2:21).

### Divine Realignment

Passover was a realignment of Israel's calendar—God said, "This month shall be for you the beginning of months" (Exodus 12:2). Everything was reordered around redemption.

**What this means now:** God is realigning His people. The shaking we see in the world is meant to shake us loose from everything that hinders—materialism, complacency, compromise—and realign us with His purposes.

### Why This Word Matters NOW

We are living in a moment when:
- Nations are raging (Psalm 2)
- Israel is surrounded (Psalm 83)
- Lawlessness is increasing (Matthew 24:12)
- Economic systems are shaking (Revelation 6)

But also:
- God is still on His throne
- His covenant with Israel is still in effect
- His purposes will not be thwarted
- His return is drawing near

The piercing, the fresh manna, the healing, the strength, the realignment—these are not abstract concepts. They are urgent realities for the hour in which we live.
