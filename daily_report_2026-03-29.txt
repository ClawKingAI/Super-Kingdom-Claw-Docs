THE MONTH OF NISAN — GOD'S NEW YEAR
We are in the 11th day of Nisan, the first month of God's calendar. In Exodus 12:2, God declared: "This month shall be for you the beginning of months. It shall be the first month of the year for you." This is not arbitrary — God set the calendar at the moment of redemption. The year begins when God redeems.

The very name "Nisan" carries prophetic meaning. In Hebrew tradition, Nisan is associated with:
• Tribe of Judah — Kingship, worship, warfare (Genesis 49:8-10)
• Letter Hei (ה) — Revelation, grace, divine breath
• The season of miracles — The Exodus, crossing the Red Sea, entering the Land

NISAN AND CURRENT EVENTS: What is God doing in this season? Israel is at war with Iran on the 11th of Nisan. In just four days, Passover begins — the commemoration of God's deliverance from Egypt. The parallel is unmistakable: Israel faces an enemy threatening destruction, yet approaches the feast of deliverance.

Historically, God has acted powerfully during Nisan:
• Abraham received covenant (traditional dating)
• The Exodus from Egypt (Exodus 12)
• The crossing of the Red Sea (Exodus 14)
• Joshua's Passover at Gilgal (Joshua 5)
• Hezekiah's Passover (2 Chronicles 30)
• Yeshua's crucifixion and resurrection (Matthew 26-28)

The pattern: God brings His people to the brink, then delivers. The current war, coinciding with Nisan, is not random. We should be watching for how God might act.

THE TORAH PORTION: PARASHAT PESACH
This week's Torah reading is Exodus 12:21-51. Key themes:
• The blood of the lamb applied to the doorposts (v. 23)
• The Lord passing over when He sees the blood (v. 27)
• No foreigner may eat of it unless circumcised (v. 43-49)
• "All the congregation of Israel shall keep it" (v. 47)

The Haftarah (Joshua 5:2-6:1, 6:27) shows the first Passover in the Land at Gilgal. The people were circumcised before observing Passover (Joshua 5:2-9). Then "the commander of the Lord's army" appeared to Joshua (Joshua 5:13-15). Then Jericho fell.

The prophetic sequence: Purification → Passover → Divine Encounter → Victory

This is the pattern for this season. Israel must remember: deliverance comes not by military might alone, but by covenant faithfulness.

THE PIERCING THAT HAS HAPPENED
The Hebrew letter associated with Nisan is Hei (ה), which means "behold" or "revelation." But prophetically, this month is also about piercing. Zechariah 12:10 prophesies: "And I will pour out on the house of David and the inhabitants of Jerusalem a spirit of grace and pleas for mercy, so that, when they look on me, on him whom they have pierced, they shall mourn for him, as one mourns for an only child."

The piercing happened at Passover. Yeshua, the Lamb of God, was pierced for our transgressions (Isaiah 53:5). In Nisan, we remember that redemption required blood. The blood of the lamb on the doorpost (Exodus 12) prefigured the blood of the Lamb on the cross.

FRESH MANNA AND FRESH FIRE
Nisan is the season of new beginnings. Just as Israel left Egypt with "unleavened bread" (Exodus 12:39), we are called to rid ourselves of the old leaven of sin (1 Corinthians 5:7-8). The "fresh manna" is the renewed word of God for this season.

In this month, ask: What old "leaven" must I remove? What fresh word is God speaking? The fire that fell at Sinai (Exodus 19) and at Pentecost (Acts 2) connects to the fire of Passover — the fire that consumed the lamb in place of the firstborn.

HEALING: HEART, MIND, BODY, SPIRIT
The Exodus was not just political deliverance — it was holistic healing. God said: "I am the Lord who heals you" (Exodus 15:26). In Nisan, we remember:
• The healing of our hearts (forgiveness for Egypt's trauma)
• The healing of our minds (renewal of identity as God's people)
• The healing of our bodies (deliverance from slavery's oppression)
• The healing of our spirits (restoration of covenant relationship)

REGAINING STRENGTH FOR HIGHER KNOWLEDGE
Deuteronomy 16:3 calls Passover "the bread of affliction" — but also the bread of liberation. In eating it, Israel remembered their affliction while being strengthened for the journey. Similarly, we are called to "examine ourselves" (1 Corinthians 11:28) so that we may be strengthened for higher things.

DIVINE REALIGNMENT
Nisan is the time for realignment. Just as Israel realigned their calendar to begin with redemption (Exodus 12:2), we realign our lives around the cross. This is the month to:
• Return to first love (Revelation 2:4-5)
• Remove what hinders (Hebrews 12:1)
• Realign priorities with God's calendar
• Reclaim identity as the redeemed

WHY THIS WORD MATTERS NOW
Israel is at war in Nisan. Passover approaches. The Church must understand: we are not spectators. We are called to pray, to intercede, to discern. The appointed times are not empty rituals — they are divine appointments. God acts in history, and He acts according to His calendar.

5. "The Month of Nisan: God's New Year" — Exodus 12:2
   God did not choose Tishrei (Rosh Hashanah) as the first month — He chose Nisan, the month of redemption. Hook: Does your calendar begin with redemption?

6. "Four Days Before Passover" — Exodus 12:3-6
   The lamb was selected on the 10th of Nisan, examined for four days, then sacrificed on the 14th. Today is the 11th of Nisan. Hook: The Lamb was examined. Are we examining ourselves?

7. "The Houthi Red Sea Threat" — Revelation 18:11-19
   The fall of Babylon involves the weeping of merchants over destroyed trade. The Red Sea crisis is an economic foretaste. Hook: Global trade is fragile. Are we putting trust in systems that will fail?
