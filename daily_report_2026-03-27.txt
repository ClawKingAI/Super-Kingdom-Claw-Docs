THE MONTH OF NISAN — MONTH OF REDEMPTION

Nisan is the FIRST month of the biblical calendar (Exodus 12:2). It is the month when God delivered Israel from Egypt through the blood of the lamb. This year, the world sits on the edge of Passover while war engulfs Persia (Iran) and Israel.

WHAT GOD MAY BE DOING IN THIS SEASON:

1. JUDGMENT ON PERSIA: The Jeremiah 49 prophecy against Elam (Persia) appears to be unfolding during the very month when Israel celebrates deliverance from another enemy (Egypt). This is not coincidence.

2. TESTING OF THE NATIONS: Strait of Hormuz closure affects every nation dependent on Gulf oil. The global economic shaking forces nations to choose sides — just as Israel was called to choose God over Egypt's gods.

3. PREPARATION FOR GREATER DELIVERANCE: If these events are "birth pangs" (Matthew 24:8), then the current shaking precedes greater deliverance. Believers must prepare spiritually, not just physically.

PROPHETIC PATTERNS FROM HISTORY:
- Passover has historically been a time of both judgment and deliverance
- The 10 plagues fell during Nisan, climaxing at Passover
- Yeshua was crucified on Passover, rose on Firstfruits
- God often acts "on time" according to His calendar

In this month of Nisan, the first month of the biblical year, God is speaking clearly:

THE PIERCING THAT HAS HAPPENED:
The Hebrew letter associated with Nisan is HEI, which means "behold" or "grace." It is formed by adding a small stroke to the letter DALET (door). This speaks of divine revelation — God opening our eyes to see what we could not see before.

This Nisan, there has been a piercing. The veil over current events has been torn. The world can no longer ignore the reality of end-times prophecy. The piercing of Israel's enemies (Iran's military infrastructure, the Strait of Hormuz blockade) mirrors a deeper piercing: God is piercing through deception, complacency, and ignorance.

FRESH MANNA AND FRESH FIRE:
Nisan is the month Israel left Egypt — leaving behind the "leeks and garlic" of Egypt for the fresh manna of the wilderness. This season, God is calling believers to exchange the old manna (old understanding, old comfort, old ways) for fresh manna — fresh revelation, fresh obedience, fresh dependence on Him.

The fire on the altar in Leviticus 6:13 was to NEVER go out. This Shabbat Tzav, let the fire be rekindled. Fresh fire for prayer. Fresh fire for intercession. Fresh fire for witness.

HEALING — HEART, MIND, BODY, SPIRIT:
The blood of the lamb in Exodus 12 brought physical protection from death, but it pointed to a greater healing — the healing of the human heart from sin. This Passover, believe God for:

- Healing of the HEART from fear, anxiety, and trauma
- Healing of the MIND from confusion and deception
- Healing of the BODY from stress-related illness
- Healing of the SPIRIT from complacency and lukewarmness

REGAINING STRENGTH FOR HIGHER KNOWLEDGE:
The current shaking is not meant to destroy believers but to STRENGTHEN them. As physical strength is built through resistance, spiritual strength is built through trial. The "higher knowledge" of God's purposes in this hour requires a strengthened spirit to receive it.

DIVINE REALIGNMENT:
Nisan is the month of new beginnings. Use this Passover season to be realigned:
- Realign your priorities (God first, kingdom first)
- Realign your time (more prayer, more Word)
- Realign your resources (generosity, preparation)
- Realign your relationships (forgiveness, reconciliation)

WHY THIS WORD MATTERS NOW:
The world is being shaken so that what cannot be shaken may remain (Hebrews 12:27). The piercing, the healing, the fresh manna, the fresh fire — these are not optional. They are essential for what is coming.

This Nisan, let Passover not be just a historical commemoration. Let it be a present reality. Apply the blood. Walk through the door. Receive fresh manna. Let the fire burn.
