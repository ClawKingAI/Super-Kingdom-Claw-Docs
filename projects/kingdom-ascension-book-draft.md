# KINGDOM ASCENSION
## From Meth to Messiah: A Journey of Transformation

**By David Morgan**

---

# PROLOGUE: The Two Trees

I stood in the Garden of Eden in my mind, and I saw them — two trees. One offered life. The other offered knowledge. One required trust. The other demanded effort. I had spent thirty years eating from the wrong tree.

The tree of the knowledge of good and evil had been my diet: my own understanding, my own striving, my own way of figuring out right from wrong. I had filled myself with the world's wisdom — the wisdom that says you are what you achieve, that identity comes from performance, that meaning is found in pleasure.

And it had left me starving.

I was a straight-A student who became a meth addict. A son who loved his father who ended up getting high with him. A young man with a future who became a repeat felon facing prison.

But then I found the other tree.

The Tree of Life. The tree that doesn't require figuring out — only trusting. The tree whose fruit is not earned but received. The tree that leads to freedom instead of captivity.

This is the story of how God took me from death to life, from prison to purpose, from striving to receiving. But more than that — this is the story of what God taught me along the way. Because the same God who pulled me out of the pit had a mountain for me to climb.

The higher I went, the more separated I became.

This book is about that ascent.

---

# PART I: THE DESCENT

## Chapter 1: Life Before the Encounter

Cumming, Georgia. A place where community and sports shaped everything. I grew up running — football fields, martial arts dojos, baseball diamonds, basketball courts. I was always moving, always pushing, always seeking the next adrenaline rush.

Skateboarding. Motocross. BMX. Paintball. Seadoos on the weekend.

I was athletic, but I was also academic. Straight-A student. Teachers loved me. Parents had expectations, and I met them. On the outside, everything looked perfect.

But inside, something was hollow.

When I hit my teenage years, the hollowness found a filling. It started with marijuana — just experimenting, just with friends, just occasionally. Everyone was doing it. What's the harm?

But "just occasionally" became "all the time." And marijuana became methamphetamines. And meth became my life.

I've been in and out of jail and prison since I was sixteen.

My father was a hard-working man — owned an Avis franchise in Alpharetta, did well for himself. He was also an alcoholic who got sober for ten years. Ten years of freedom. Until he fell and broke his ribs, and the doctor prescribed medication, and the medication led him back to the bottle.

And then he started using with me. Cocaine. Xanax. Father and son, getting high together on the drive home from work.

I didn't know it then, but I was learning a spiritual law: the sins of the fathers visit the children. Generational iniquity was flowing like a river through my bloodline, and I was drowning in it.

But even in the darkest moments, God was leaving breadcrumbs.

I would read the Bible out loud — especially Revelation. Every time someone gave an altar call, I was there, saying yes to Jesus, believing He died for my sins and rose again. I'd feel peace for a moment, a sense that maybe I was saved.

But no one discipled me. No one taught me what it meant to be a new creation. I believed I was saved, but I didn't know for sure if I'd go to heaven or hell.

I was eating from the Tree of Knowledge — knowing *about* God without *knowing* God.

And the fruit of that tree was death.

---

## Chapter 2: The Wrong Tree

Let me tell you about the two trees.

It wasn't until years later that I understood what had been happening to me all along. In the Garden of Eden, God placed two trees at the center. One was the Tree of Life. The other was the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil.

Most people think the Tree of Knowledge was about sin. But it wasn't just about sin — it was about *self*. It was about deciding for yourself what is right and wrong, good and evil. It was about independence from God, reliance on your own understanding, living by your own wisdom.

That was my life.

I was a straight-A student because I trusted my intellect. I used drugs because I trusted my desires. I ran from consequences because I trusted my ability to escape.

Every choice I made was from the wrong tree.

**Truth vs. Deception**

The Tree of Knowledge is rooted in deception. The serpent told Eve, "You will not surely die. Your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God."

I believed the same lie. I thought drugs would open my eyes. I thought I could be like God — controlling my reality, escaping my pain, finding my own way.

But the way of the Tree of Knowledge leads to death. Proverbs 14:12 says, "There is a way that seems right to a man, but its end is the way of death."

My way seemed right. I was surviving. I was functioning. I was still me.

But I was dying.

**Receiving vs. Striving**

Here's what I didn't understand: the Tree of Life requires receiving. The Tree of Knowledge requires striving.

The Tree of Life says, "Trust God. Receive from Him. Rest in His provision."

The Tree of Knowledge says, "Figure it out. Earn it. Achieve it."

I was a striver. I earned my grades. I earned my athletic achievements. I thought I had to earn everything — even salvation. I'd go to the altar, get saved, try harder, fail, and repeat.

I didn't know that 1 Corinthians 4:7 asks, "What do you have that you did not receive? If then you received it, why do you boast as if you did not receive it?"

I was boasting in my straight-A's, my athletic ability, my survival skills. But none of it saved me. None of it could.

I needed the other tree.

# PART II: THE TURNING

## Chapter 3: A Divine Encounter

Rock bottom has a way of focusing your attention.

I was back in jail, facing the reality that I was likely going to prison for a long time. My lawyer told me there was little hope. The charges were serious. My record was worse.

But God had a better way.

Literally — a program called "A Better Way."

I was skeptical. I was resistant. I'd been to programs before. I'd tried to change before. But something about this was different.

See, the court had actually released me by mistake. I thought I'd gotten off. But when I called my lawyer, she told me the police were looking for me. I had two choices: run or turn myself in.

I ran.

When I finally got caught, my lawyer said I'd ruined my chances. The deal was gone. The book was going to be thrown at me.

But the court showed mercy. Because the mistake was theirs — they'd released me — they didn't worsen my punishment. They gave me what I didn't deserve: another chance at A Better Way.

That was the first time I understood grace.

Grace is getting what you don't deserve. I deserved prison. I got a program. I deserved punishment. I got mercy.

It was a picture of the gospel.

But I didn't see it yet.

---

## Chapter 4: The Veil Lifts

The first weeks at A Better Way, I was still running. Just internally instead of externally.

I tried to manipulate my way out. I wrote a letter to my mom, hoping she'd help me escape. The staff read it and called me out.

I tried to leave. Called my dad, expecting support. Instead, he said, "You're trying to run your own program."

My mom didn't answer the phone.

And in that moment of abandonment, something happened. I felt the Holy Spirit speak to me for the first time I could remember:

*"You don't want to leave Me here."*

That was it. Seven words. But they broke me.

I asked if I could stay. I called Sir Phil, one of the mentors. He said, "He wants to stay now. Let him stay."

That decision — to stay — marked the beginning of my transformation.

It took about four months for the veil to lift. Four months of teaching, of mentoring, of the Word chipping away at my hardened heart.

And then came the deliverance conference at CCC Church in Newnan.

---

## Chapter 5: Baptism of Fire

They taught about the Baptism of the Holy Spirit at that conference. I'd grown up not believing the gifts of the Spirit were for today. But I was desperate enough to try anything.

They gave an invitation to come forward to receive. I went straight up.

Sharon Mullins was there — she'd prophesied over me before, writing down my name without knowing me. I trusted her.

She grabbed my hand and said, "You already have it."

I was frustrated. I didn't *feel* any different. She told me to go sit down and practice speaking in tongues.

So I did. I sat down, closed my eyes, and started speaking. It felt like there was a line of information in the back of my head, so I spoke it out.

A lady came up and put her finger on my stomach. She said, "It's like a new sword."

And suddenly, my syllables became crisp. Clear. A language I didn't know was flowing from my lips.

My life changed dramatically from that moment.

I began to understand God's Word more effectively. My faith was powerfully strengthened. I now pray in tongues every day — several times a day, often for more than an hour.

Here's what I learned: the Baptism of the Holy Spirit isn't about a feeling. It's about faith. You receive it the same way you receive salvation — by believing and asking.

The Holy Spirit gave me a prayer language — a perfect prayer, an invincible weapon in spiritual warfare, a deeper intimacy with Jesus.

I had found the Tree of Life. And it was better than anything I'd ever tasted.

---

## Chapter 6: Understanding Covenant

At A Better Way, I met John Barrow. He taught about the covenant of Abraham, and it changed everything.

I'd never understood covenant. I thought Christianity was about trying to be good and hoping God would accept me. I didn't know that God had made binding, unbreakable promises — to Abraham, to Isaac, to Jacob, and now to me through Jesus.

John explained three covenants:

1. **The Abrahamic Covenant** — God's unconditional promise of blessing, land, and descendants. A covenant of grace, not law.

2. **The Sinaitic Covenant** — The law given at Mount Sinai. Conditional blessing based on obedience. This covenant showed us our need for a Savior because we could never keep it perfectly.

3. **The New Covenant** — Jesus fulfilled the law and established a better covenant, based on better promises (Hebrews 8:6). Through His blood, we're grafted into Israel's inheritance (Romans 11).

This was revolutionary.

I didn't have to earn God's favor. I already had it through covenant. I didn't have to strive for acceptance. I was accepted.

The Tree of Knowledge asks, "What must I do?"
The Tree of Life answers, "It is finished."

---

## Chapter 7: The Power of the Holy Spirit

Through A Better Way, I was introduced to Joel Crumpton and his teaching on the power of the Holy Spirit.

His book, *You Can Do the Works of Jesus*, shattered my religious box. I thought miracles were for Bible times. Joel showed me from Scripture that the works of Jesus weren't confined to the past — they were for today.

More than that: Jesus said we would do *greater works* (John 14:12).

Joel taught us to move from theoretical belief to practical demonstration. He introduced us to "The Ultimate Treasure Hunt" — an outreach where you ask the Holy Spirit for specific clues (location, name, appearance, need, unusual identifier) and then go find the person God highlights.

The first time I did this, I was terrified. What if I heard wrong? What if I looked foolish? What if nothing happened?

But Joel said faith is easier to act into believing than to believe into acting. So I acted.

I got my clues. I stepped out. And I found the person — exactly as God had shown.

When I ministered to them, God's power showed up. Healing. Deliverance. Salvation.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "But did it really work?"
The Tree of Life says, "I saw, I obeyed, I witnessed."

# PART III: THE ASCENT

## Chapter 8: Ascending to the Sacred

I began to understand that God was calling me higher.

There's a pattern in Scripture that I'd never seen before. Israel was called out from the nations. The Levites were called out from Israel. The Kohanim (priests) were called out from the Levites. The High Priest was called out from the priests.

Each level of calling required a deeper level of separation.

The greater the calling, the deeper the consecration.

And here's what hit me: in Christ, we're called to all of it. 1 Peter 2:9 says we're "a royal priesthood, a holy nation." We have access to the Holy of Holies through Jesus.

But access doesn't mean automatic entry. We still have to choose to ascend.

We still have to separate.

For me, that separation looked like:

- Separating from old friends who pulled me back
- Separating from old habits that opened doors to the enemy
- Separating from old mindsets that kept me striving
- Separating from comfort zones that limited God's power

The Tree of Life demands everything. But it gives everything in return.

---

## Chapter 9: The Prophet, Priest, and King

As I grew, God began to show me my identity in Him. I wasn't just a saved sinner. I was a prophet. A priest. A king.

**Prophet** — A messenger of God, carrying His heart, speaking His word. In the Old Testament, prophets called people back to faithfulness and foretold future events. In Christ, we're all called to speak God's truth and encourage others (Acts 2:17-18).

**Priest** — A mediator between God and people, offering sacrifices and prayers. In the Old Testament, priests had to be from the tribe of Levi. In Christ, we're all priests (Revelation 1:6), offering spiritual sacrifices and interceding for others.

**King** — A ruler who governs, protects, and provides. In the Old Testament, kings had to be from the line of David. In Christ, we reign with Him (Revelation 5:10), exercising authority over the enemy and stewarding God's creation.

But here's what I learned: these offices have boundaries.

Nadab and Abihu offered "strange fire" and were consumed by God's judgment (Leviticus 10). King Uzziah tried to burn incense and was struck with leprosy (2 Chronicles 26). Uzzah touched the Ark and died (2 Samuel 6).

God is holy. His offices are sacred. We can't just do whatever we want.

But David showed me something else. He functioned as prophet, priest, and king — even though he wasn't supposed to. He ate the showbread. He wore the ephod. He wrote prophetic psalms.

How? Because his heart was after God. And Jesus later referenced David to show that mercy triumphs over sacrifice (Matthew 12:3-4).

Jesus is the ultimate fulfillment of all three offices. And in Him, we share in His anointing.

But we must walk in humility and holiness.

---

## Chapter 10: Spiritual Realities

At some point, I realized I wasn't just fighting flesh and blood.

I'd grown up thinking the spiritual realm was abstract — maybe even imaginary. But the more I walked with God, the more I saw it.

There were two interconnected dimensions: physical and spiritual. Everything visible came from something invisible (Hebrews 11:3). The physical world was birthed from the spiritual.

This changed everything.

My thoughts weren't just thoughts — they were spiritual seeds.
My words weren't just words — they carried life or death (Proverbs 18:21).
My actions weren't just actions — they opened or closed doors in the spirit.

I began to understand why I'd been trapped for so long. I'd been fighting the wrong battle. I thought my enemy was the police, the court system, my circumstances. But Ephesians 6:12 says our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "Fix your circumstances."
The Tree of Life says, "Address the spiritual root."

And I had roots.

---

## Chapter 11: Rebellion as Witchcraft

God began to show me things about my bloodline that I'd never known.

1 Samuel 15:23 says, "For rebellion is as the sin of witchcraft."

I'd always thought witchcraft was about spells and potions. But God showed me that witchcraft is about seeking power, knowledge, or influence through supernatural means — bypassing God's authority.

And rebellion is the same thing.

Both stem from self-reliance. Both say, "I will determine my own way." Both place human wisdom above divine instruction.

I had walked in rebellion my whole life. I'd broken God's commandments, chosen my own path, defied His authority.

That rebellion had given the enemy legal rights in my life.

But there was more.

---

## Chapter 12: Breaking Generational Curses

I learned about ancestral altars — spiritual contracts made by my forefathers that gave demons legal access to my life.

My ancestors had sinned. They'd built altars to false gods. They'd made covenants with demonic powers. They'd practiced witchcraft, idolatry, sexual immorality, and every form of iniquity.

These sins opened doors for curses: poverty, sickness, premature death, addiction, mental illness.

I had to do something I'd never done before: I had to repent on behalf of my bloodline.

I stood in the gap for my father's house and my mother's house. I confessed these sins before God. I renounced every ancestral altar. I renounced every covenant made with demons.

I uprooted every generational altar by the authority of Jesus.

This wasn't emotional. It was legal.

In the Courts of Heaven, I presented my case. I acknowledged the sin. I applied the blood of Jesus. I received the verdict: NOT GUILTY.

The curses were broken.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "You are what your family made you."
The Tree of Life says, "You are who God says you are."

# PART IV: THE EMPOWERMENT

## Chapter 13: Divine Decrees

I learned that my words matter more than I knew.

Isaiah 55:11 says God's Word doesn't return empty. Job 22:28 says, "You will also decree a thing, and it will be established for you."

Divine decrees are authoritative statements rooted in God's Word that we declare over our lives. They're not positive thinking. They're not name-it-and-claim-it. They're agreeing with what God has already said.

I began to decree:

**Protection:** "No weapon formed against me shall prosper" (Isaiah 54:17).

**Provision:** "The full cost of the vision God has placed in my heart will be paid from the King's revenue" (Philippians 4:19).

**Daily Supply:** "All my needs will be met each day without fail" (Matthew 6:11).

**Legal Right:** "Every prophetic word and vision God has given me is a legal document in the Courts of Heaven" (Jeremiah 1:12).

These weren't wishes. They were verdicts. I was enforcing what had already been established in heaven.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "I hope things work out."
The Tree of Life says, "It is written."

---

## Chapter 14: Understanding God's Rewards

I also learned that how I live matters for eternity.

There are two keys to our eternal future: belief and behavior.

Belief determines where we spend eternity. Behavior determines our experience in eternity.

Scripture describes two judgments:

1. **The Judgment Seat of Christ** (for believers) — where our works are evaluated and rewards are given (2 Corinthians 5:10).

2. **The Great White Throne Judgment** (for unbelievers) — where those not in the Book of Life are judged and cast into the lake of fire (Revelation 20:11-15).

As believers, we're not judged for salvation — that's secure. But we're judged for rewards.

What we do here echoes forever.

I began to live with an eternal perspective. Every act of faithfulness, every sacrifice, every moment of suffering for Christ — it all matters. God sees, and God rewards.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "Live for now."
The Tree of Life says, "Live for then."

---

## Chapter 15: The Appointed Times

God also taught me about His calendar.

In Leviticus 23, God established seven feasts — appointed times — that tell the story of Messiah. Through Jesus' blood, we're grafted into Israel's inheritance, and these feasts become our heritage.

The Feasts of the Lord narrate Jesus' story:
- Passover — His death
- Unleavened Bread — His burial
- Firstfruits — His resurrection
- Pentecost — His Spirit
- Trumpets — His return
- Atonement — His judgment
- Tabernacles — His dwelling

We don't observe them for salvation. We observe them from salvation — as a joyful invitation to meet with God during His appointed times.

These times are set apart for breakthroughs, healing, guidance, and realignment.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "Every day is the same."
The Tree of Life says, "There are set times for encounter."

---

## Chapter 16: A Divine Detour to Washington

I want to tell you about one specific day that changed everything.

I hadn't planned to go to the "Let Us Worship" event on the National Mall in Washington, D.C. I was enjoying a quiet Saturday, worshiping the Lord in my room at A Better Way.

But then I felt a prompting: *Go.*

I resisted. It would be expensive. Inconvenient. I told the Lord, "I'm good. I can worship You here."

But then I asked for a sign: if He wanted me there, let me know someone who was already going.

Immediately, Jeff and Yana Bisiglio came to mind. I called Jeff. He said Yana was there. I called Yana. She confirmed.

So I went.

I booked a flight that day. When I arrived, I rented a white Toyota truck. When I turned on my camera, the screen lit up with an inexplicable bright light — even though the sky was overcast.

At the hotel, they gave me room 412.

412. Isaiah 41:2? Or was God saying something about April 12? I didn't know, but I knew I was meant to be there.

The next morning, thousands of us gathered at the Supreme Court steps. It was raining. We sang "I Exalt Thee" with umbrellas in hand, praying specifically for the overturning of Roe v. Wade.

Throughout the day, I kept crossing paths with a revivalist named Josh Lindquist. We were busy with water baptisms. I had the privilege of baptizing several people — mostly children with their mothers.

Random people came up to me, prophesying over my future. One even mentioned a YouTube channel — which I hadn't started yet.

At the end, a pastor approached me. She invited us to an Eagles meeting for revivalists the next day.

I went.

The room felt alive. Pastor Songhee Bae led the meeting. When it was my turn to share my vision, the Spirit of the Lord filled me, and I spoke about helping people exalt God's Word over their experiences.

That moment — that divine appointment — set the trajectory for everything that came next.

The Tree of Knowledge says, "Plan your life."
The Tree of Life says, "Follow the cloud."

# PART V: THE ACTIVATION

## Chapter 17: The Prophetic Gift

After Washington, I leaned into prophecy.

CCC Church had prophetic tables after service — four people would prophesy over you. I did it every chance I got. The leadership told us to record the prophecies so we could test them and wage warfare with them.

I have hours of recorded prophecies now. Many have already come to pass.

Through prophecy, I knew God saw me. He knew me. He loved me. And He had a vision for my life.

1 Timothy 1:18 says to wage the good warfare with the prophecies spoken over you. I took that seriously.

I discovered Bethel Atlanta Church and took their prophetic equipping class. Melissa Healy taught us how to give prophetic words rooted in intimacy with the Lord.

During that class, Melissa prophesied over me: I would go from city to city, state to state, ushering in revival.

That word set a new trajectory.

---

## Chapter 18: The Eagles Meeting

I reconnected with Songhee Bae when she came to Georgia. We gathered at the Daniel Prayer Garden — a Korean-style prayer retreat with a hundred beds and small prayer huts.

The Spirit of prophecy was powerfully present. I found myself receiving words for everyone we prophesied over.

Night watches. Prayer huts. The presence of God thick in that place.

I met Timothy Price, one of the pastors of Freedom Chapel. I connected with ministries I'd never heard of. God was knitting together a network.

And He was preparing me for the next level.

# EPILOGUE: The Invitation

I started this book in a jail cell, facing prison.

I'm finishing it as a son of God, walking in prophetic authority, helping others find the same freedom I found.

But I'm not done ascending. There's more.

The same God who pulled me out of meth addiction is pulling me deeper into consecration. The same Holy Spirit who gave me a prayer language is teaching me to war with it. The same Jesus who saved me is calling me to co-reign with Him.
